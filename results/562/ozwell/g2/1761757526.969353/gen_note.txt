**Clinical Note:**

**Patient Information:**
- Young male, fit and healthy
- No relevant medical history or known allergies
- Surgical history: Orthognathic surgery 1.5 years ago

**Chief Complaint:**
- Pain and swelling on the right cheek for the past three months

**History of Present Illness:**
- Patient has been experiencing recurrent pain and swelling on the right cheek
- Previous treatment with analgesics and antibiotics by a general practitioner was ineffective
- During orthognathic surgery, an orthodontic appliance was dislodged into the right cheek
- Surgical team initially opted for clinical observation due to postoperative facial oedema and absence of symptoms during follow-up

**Examination:**
- No extraoral swelling observed
- Mandible and maxilla are firm
- Sinus track with slight pus discharge noted on the upper right buccal sulcus region
- Tenderness present upon palpation of the affected area

**Assessment:**
- Suspected that the sinus track originates from the dislodged orthodontic appliance embedded in the cheek soft tissue
- Periapical view confirmed the presence of the dislodged appliance causing pain and swelling

**Plan:**
- Schedule an appointment for removal of the dislodged appliance
- Follow-up appointment in one week
- Advise patient to keep the area clean and avoid strenuous activities

**Follow-Up:**
- Patient to return in one week for follow-up after appliance removal

**Instructions to Patient:**
- Maintain oral hygiene
- Avoid strenuous activities until follow-up appointment

**Next Steps:**
- Removal of dislodged appliance
- Monitor healing and resolution of symptoms during follow-up appointment
