**Subject:** Thoracic Wall Tumor – Follow-Up

**History of Presenting Illness:** Patient presents today reporting persistent dull pain in the right back and a swelling in the right hemithorax for several weeks.

**Examination Findings:** Patient reports good overall health and is very active.

**Past Medical History:** Patient reports a history of a simple fracture of the 9th right rib, sustained one year prior to this presentation, due to a thoracic trauma at work.

**Imaging:**
*   Initial CT scan revealed a 8 x 4 cm thoracic wall tumor with heterogeneous density, involving and destructing the 9th rib and extending into two adjacent intercostal spaces.
*   CT scan also showed micronodular infiltrations of both lungs with diameters up to 5 mm and a single nodule in the lower lobe of the right lung measuring approximately 1 cm in diameter. The mediastinum was without enlarged lymph nodes. 

**Diagnostic Procedure & Results:**
*   Following the imaging findings, a frozen biopsy of the lung lesions and an excision biopsy of the thoracic wall tumor were performed.
*   Biopsy results were negative for malignant cells.
*   Due to negative biopsy results, an oncologic resection of the tumor of the thoracic wall was performed, involving the resection of three ribs, including the 8th and 9th ribs. 

**Plan:** Patient requires regular follow-up to monitor for recurrence.
