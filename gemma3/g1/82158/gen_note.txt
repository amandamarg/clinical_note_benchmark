**Subject:** Urinary Symptoms – Possible IUD Complications

**History of Presenting Illness:** Patient presents today reporting urinary symptoms of dysuria and intermittent hematuria experienced for the past 2 years.

**Past Medical History:** Patient reports a history of an intrauterine device (IUD) insertion 9 years ago, followed by a normal vaginal delivery 3 months later. A second IUD was inserted 40 days postpartum and remained in place for 6 years until its removal due to persistent urinary symptoms. 

**Physical Examination:** Physical examination at initial presentation revealed no remarkable findings. 

**Laboratory Findings:** Urinalysis revealed pyuria and hematuria. Urine culture was negative.

**Imaging Findings:**
*   Abdominal Ultrasonography: Demonstrated an echogenic intravesical lesion measuring approximately 27 mm with a distal acoustic shadow, suggestive of a foreign body or calculus.
*   Plain Radiography of the Pelvis: Non-informative regarding details of a foreign body.

**Diagnosis:** Partially embedded IUD on the posterior bladder wall complicated with stone formation. 

**Treatment:** Patient underwent endoscopic surgery to remove the stone around the IUD.
