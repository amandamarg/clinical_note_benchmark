**Clinical Note**

**Patient Name:** [Patient's Name]  
**Date:** 10/1/2025  
**Physician:** Dr. Smith  

**Chief Complaint:**  
Injury to both thighs with severe pain and inability to stand.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient was injured during field training, experiencing severe continuous pain in both thighs, which worsened with movement. Tenderness and swelling were noted. Initial management by the trainer included compression dressing and ice packs. The patient was transported to the clinic by ambulance.

**Physical Examination:**  
- Swelling present on both thighs.  
- Pain exacerbated by pressure on quadriceps muscle and knee movement.  
- Inability to perform isometric quadriceps contraction with knee in full extension due to pain.  
- Active knee range of movement: 40° (right), 55° (left).  
- Passive range of movement is the same due to pain.  
- Peripheral artery pulse normal as assessed by Doppler ultrasound.

**Diagnostic Tests:**  
- **Roentgenogram:** Negative for fracture; patella not displaced.  
- **Ultrasonography:** Haematoma formation on both rectus femoris muscles.  
- **Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI):** Haematomas within the muscles with diameters of 6 cm (left) and 5 cm (right).

**Assessment:**  
- Second-grade (moderate) muscle strains of the rectus femoris muscles with haematoma formation.  
- No evidence of compartment syndrome.  
- Coagulation profile normal; no bleeding detected.

**Plan:**  
- Rest and avoid physical activity for a few days.  
- Prescribe painkillers for pain management.  
- Re-evaluation scheduled in one week.

**Patient Education:**  
The patient was advised to rest and avoid any physical activity to allow for healing. Pain management strategies were discussed.

**Follow-Up:**  
The patient will be re-evaluated in one week to assess recovery progress and adjust the treatment plan as necessary.
