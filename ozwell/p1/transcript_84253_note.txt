**Patient Information:**  
- **Age:** Not specified

**Chief Complaint:**  
Mass in the genital area causing itching, difficulty walking, and having sex.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient reports a mass in the left vulvar inguinal region that has been present since puberty. Initially a small papillary formation, it has grown rapidly in recent years. The mass causes significant itching, pain, and difficulty with sexual intercourse, which has affected her family life as she and her husband have not had a child in the past 5 years. The patient was circumcised by a village midwife at age 7 and has had three vaginal deliveries at home since marrying at age 16.

**Physical Examination:**  
- Mass: 4 × 3 × 2 cm, regularly contoured, mobile, sensitive, covered with normal skin, located at the left vulvar inguinal region.  
- The mass causes difficulty in walking and sitting.

**Diagnostic Tests:**  
- Complete blood count, complete urine analysis, and biochemistry parameters: Within normal limits.  
- Ultrasound: Benign appearing mass of cystic nature without lobulation and septation.

**Assessment and Plan:**  
- Diagnosis: Benign cystic mass in the left vulvar inguinal region.
- Plan: Recommend total mass excision operation under general anesthesia, expected to take about an hour. Post-operative care includes rest for a few days, avoiding strenuous activities, and a follow-up appointment in a week.
- The excised mass will be sent to the laboratory for microscopy to determine the exact nature and composition of the cyst.

**Patient Instructions:**  
- Rest for a few days post-operation.
- Avoid any strenuous activities.
- Return for a follow-up appointment in one week to assess recovery progress.
