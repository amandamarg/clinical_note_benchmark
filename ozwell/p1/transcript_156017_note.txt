**Clinical Note**

**Patient Name:** [Patient's Name]  
**Date:** 10/1/2025  
**Physician:** [Doctor's Name]  

**Chief Complaint:**  
Swelling on the right side of the jaw with discharge.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient reports swelling on the right side of the jaw that began approximately one month ago. There is also discharge present. The patient has a history of a motor vehicle accident three months ago, during which they sustained a fracture on the right side of the jaw.

**Examination:**  
- Swelling of 1 x 1.5 cm on the right side of the jaw with a crusty appearance on the overlying skin.  
- Sinus opening with pus discharge noted.  
- Interdental wiring from 73 to 83 observed.  
- Dental abnormalities: Missing 31, rotated 41, 42, and 32, anterior open bite, and posterior open bite on the left side.

**Radiographic Findings:**  
- Orthopantomogram shows a bone plate with 4 screws in the region of 43 and 45, oriented obliquely.  
- Fracture line on the right side of the jaw extending from the interdental area of 42 and 83 vertically and horizontally along the lower border of the jaw.  
- Step deformity in the lower border of the jaw indicating improperly reduced fracture.  
- Malunion of the subcondylar fracture on the left side.

**Diagnosis:**  
Malunited fractures of the body and the condyle of the mandible on opposite sides.

**Treatment:**  
- Under local anesthesia, interdental wiring was removed.  
- Crevicular incision made from 41 to 46 to expose the fracture site.  
- Screws and bone plate were carefully retrieved.  
- Prophylactic extraction of 83, 84, and 85 performed to facilitate the eruption of underlying permanent teeth and evaluate potential damage from bone plating.

**Post-Operative Care:**  
The post-operative period was uneventful. The patient is advised to return for follow-up in 45 days.

**Plan:**  
Monitor healing and eruption of permanent teeth. Evaluate for any further intervention if necessary.

**Follow-Up:**  
Patient to return in 45 days for follow-up evaluation.
