**Clinical Note**

**Patient Information:**  
- **Condition:** Left parietal calvarial osteomyelitis due to actinomycosis  
- **History:** Presented in the puerperal period with no extracranial focus. Initial episode in 2010 involved osteomyelitis of the outer table of calvarium. Adequate surgical excision was performed, but the patient did not comply with antimicrobial therapy and was lost to follow-up. Relapse occurred after 11 years, with the lesion penetrating the inner table and extending to the epidural space. No brain parenchymal involvement was noted.

**Symptoms:**  
- Pain on the left side of the head  
- Headache  
- Fever  

**Treatment:**  
- Combination of surgery and antibiotics  
- Penicillin is the drug of choice for actinomycosis, to be taken for a prolonged period of 6 to 12 months.

**Notes:**  
The patient responded well to the combination of surgery and antibiotics. It is crucial for the patient to comply with the prolonged penicillin therapy to ensure full treatment of the infection. The patient is advised to reach out with any questions or concerns.
