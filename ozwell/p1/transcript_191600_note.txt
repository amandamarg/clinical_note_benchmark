**Clinical Note**

**Date:** 10/1/2025  
**Time:** 7:36:29 PM  

**Patient Information:**  
- **Symptoms:** The patient presented with a crawling sensation and foreign body sensation in the right eye. No prior symptoms were reported. No systemic symptoms or signs aside from ocular complaints. No swelling or itching noted. Visual acuity was 6/6 in both eyes.  

**History:**  
- The patient has a history of working in a cocoa farm plantation during childhood and suffered from bites from unknown flies during those years.  

**Examination:**  
- Ocular examination revealed the presence of an actively mobile worm in the nasal subconjunctival space of the right eye. The patient reported a crawling sensation during the examination and again after adopting a face-down position.  

**Procedure:**  
- The patient was taken to the operating room where the worm was successfully extracted using a local infiltration of the conjunctiva with lignocaine anesthesia.  

**Diagnosis:**  
- Histological examination identified the worm as an adult Loa loa.  

**Plan:**  
- Continue monitoring for any further symptoms or complications. Provide patient education on Loa loa and potential follow-up care.  

**Family Discussion:**  
- Informed the patient's family about the findings and the successful extraction of the worm.
