- **Chief Complaint:** Mass on left ring finger

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient presents with a mass on the left ring finger, which appeared suddenly 7 months ago without any history of trauma. The patient underwent incision and drainage 2 months after the mass appeared, but there was no improvement. The patient is concerned about the possibility of malignancy.

**Past Medical History:**  
- No significant past medical or surgical history.

**Family and Drug History:**  
- Unremarkable.

**Physical Examination:**  
- Mass located on the ulnar side of the proximal phalanx of the left ring finger, extensively involving the 4th web space.
- Overlying skin coverage is ulcerative with no active signs of infection.
- Limited range of motion in the involved digit.
- Neurovascular examination is normal.

**Radiologic Evaluation:**  
- Soft tissue swelling with no evidence of bone involvement.

**Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI):**  
- Mass on the volar aspect of the ring finger encasing about 50% of the flexor tendons.
- Low signal intensity on T1 and high signal intensity on T2 evaluation with strong enhancement in post-contrast evaluation.
- Partial abutment of the radial sided bundle and complete encirclement of the ulnar sided neurovascular bundle.
- Surrounding bone free of any masses and associated mass effect.

**Surgical Intervention:**  
- Patient taken to the OR for exploration and mass excision.
- Bruner type incision used.
- Subcutaneous mass with fibro-fatty consistency and extensive fascia-like extension to surrounding soft tissue.
- Mass encircling the ulnar neurovascular bundle with mass abutment over the radial bundle.
- Mass dissected freely.

**Plan:**  
- Discussed possible risks associated with the intervention with the patient.
- Follow-up appointment scheduled in two weeks.
