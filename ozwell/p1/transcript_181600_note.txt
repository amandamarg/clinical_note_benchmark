**Clinical Note**

**Patient Name:** [Patient's Name]  
**Date:** 10/1/2025  
**Time:** 7:33 PM  

**Chief Complaint:**  
Crawling sensation and foreign body sensation in the right eye.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient presented with a sudden onset of a crawling sensation and foreign body sensation in the right eye. The patient reported no prior symptoms before the onset. There is a history of working in a cocoa farm plantation during childhood years, with exposure to bites from unknown flies. The patient denies any systemic symptoms, swelling, or itching elsewhere in the body.

**Review of Systems:**  
- Ocular: Crawling sensation in the right eye, no changes in visual acuity (6/6 in both eyes).
- Systemic: No systemic symptoms reported.

**Physical Examination:**  
- Ocular Examination: Presence of an actively mobile worm in the nasal subconjunctival space of the right eye. The patient reported a crawling sensation during the examination.

**Procedures:**  
The patient was taken to the operating room where the worm was successfully extracted using local infiltration of the conjunctiva with lignocaine anesthesia.

**Histological Findings:**  
Histological examination identified the extracted worm as an adult Loa loa.

**Plan:**  
- Monitor for any further symptoms or complications.
- Educate the patient and family about Loa loa and potential follow-up care.

**Family Discussion:**  
The patient's family was informed about the findings and the successful extraction of the worm. They were advised on the nature of the Loa loa worm and the importance of monitoring for any additional symptoms.
