**Chief Complaint:**  
Right ear pain for the past 6 months.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient reports experiencing significant pain in the right ear for the past 6 months. Initially, there was clear fluid discharge from the right ear, which became serosanguineous over the last three months. The patient has been using ciprodex and keflex without relief. The patient denies any history of smoking or alcohol abuse.

**Review of Systems:**  
- Decreased sensation over the right side of the face in the second and third trigeminal region.
- Ptosis and paralysis of the right eye.
- Inability to protrude the tongue.

**Physical Examination:**  
- Right ear: External auditory canal is edematous with clear discharge and bulging of the tympanic membrane.

**Imaging Studies:**  
- **CT Scan:** Opacification of the right external auditory canal and bony destructive changes of the anterior wall of the right external auditory canal, right tympanic and epitympanic spaces, and mastoid antrum.
- **MRI Scan:** Extensive heterogeneous signal abnormality and enhancement centered about the right external auditory canal and middle ear region. Equivocal 7mm fluid collection in the carotid space and associated dural enhancement in the sigmoid sinus.

**Assessment:**  
The findings are consistent with a severe case of otitis media.

**Plan:**  
- Initiate treatment with antibiotics.
- Follow up with the ear, nose, and throat clinic as an outpatient.

**Follow-Up:**  
The patient was readmitted three weeks later with symptoms of nausea and vomiting and worsening of the right ear pain. A re-examination is planned to assess any changes in the patient's condition.
