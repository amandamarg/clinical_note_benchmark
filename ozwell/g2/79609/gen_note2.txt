**Clinical Note:**

**Date:** 10/8/2025  
**Time:** 5:43:24 PM  

**Patient Information:**  
- **Patient:** Male child  
- **Accompanied by:** Mother  

**Chief Complaint:**  
- Referred by pediatric cardiologist for evaluation of lung condition.

**History of Present Illness:**  
- The patient has been experiencing vague upper respiratory symptoms, food regurgitation, and frequent vomiting over the past seven months. No solid food dysphagia reported, but increased drooling noted.

**Diagnostic Findings:**  
- Chest radiograph revealed a round metallic object in the upper esophagus with an irregular contour, causing a mild mass effect on the left aspect of the trachea.
- Barium swallow indicated normal swallowing without difficulty; foreign body separate from the esophageal tract.

**Procedure:**  
- Esophagoscopy performed in the operating room. The patient was hemodynamically stable with normal oxygen saturation. Examination revealed mild crackles over lung bases, no stridor or hoarseness.
- Intraoperatively, the foreign body was not visualized, but a hard mass was palpated at the lateral esophageal wall, covered by granulation tissue.
- Multiple attempts to remove the foreign body were unsuccessful. Surgery was aborted, and an external approach was planned.

**Post-Operative Care:**  
- The patient was transferred to the pediatric intensive care unit for close monitoring and necessary care.

**Plan:**  
- Continue monitoring in the pediatric intensive care unit.
- Update the patient's mother on progress and further management plans.
