**Clinical Note:**

**Patient Information:**
- Young male patient with no relevant medical history or known allergies.

**Chief Complaint:**
- Pain and swelling on the right cheek for the past three months.

**History of Present Illness:**
- The patient has been experiencing recurrent pain and swelling on the right cheek.
- Previous consultations with a general practitioner resulted in prescriptions for analgesics and antibiotics, which provided temporary relief but symptoms recurred.

**Past Surgical History:**
- Orthognathic surgery performed one and a half years ago.
- During the surgery, an orthodontic appliance was dislodged into the right cheek.
- The surgical team opted for clinical observation due to pronounced postoperative facial oedema and absence of symptoms during follow-up.

**Examination:**
- No extraoral swelling observed.
- Mandible and maxilla are firm.
- Presence of a sinus track with slight pus discharge in the upper right buccal sulcus region.
- Tenderness noted upon palpation of the affected area.

**Imaging:**
- Periapical view confirms the presence of a dislodged orthodontic appliance embedded in the cheek soft tissue.

**Assessment:**
- The dislodged orthodontic appliance is causing the pain and swelling.

**Plan:**
- Schedule an appointment for the removal of the dislodged appliance.
- Follow-up appointment in one week.
- Advise the patient to keep the area clean and avoid strenuous activities until the follow-up.
