**Chief Complaint:**  
Progressive trismus with difficulty in chewing and limited mouth opening.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient reports a few years' history of progressive trismus, which has worsened recently. The patient experiences difficulty chewing food and limited mouth opening.

**Medical History:**  
- Hypertension (controlled with medication)  
- Hypercholesterolemia (controlled with medication)  
- Benign prostatic hyperplasia (controlled with medication)  
- History of chewing pan of Indian tobacco for the last 15 years

**Examination:**  
- No lymphadenopathy, swelling, or asymmetry observed.  
- Limited mouth opening at 15 mm.  
- Altered sensation in the buccal branch of V3 bilaterally.  
- Intraoral exam revealed poor oral hygiene and multiple carious teeth.  
- Severe fibrous bands in buccal mucosa; soft palate, tongue, and floor of mouth not involved.  
- Pale-appearing mucosa due to fibrosis.

**Diagnosis:**  
Submucous fibrosis infiltrating the muscular layer, confirmed by incisional biopsy and histopathological evaluation.

**Treatment Plan:**  
- Surgical excision of fibrous bands using a transoral approach.  
- Induction via intravenous general anesthesia and intubation without complications.  
- Local anesthesia infiltration in surgical areas.  
- Surgical dissection to the level of the buccinator muscle, with bleeding control and wound edge preparation for graft placement.
