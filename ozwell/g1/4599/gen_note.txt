**Chief Complaint:**  
Crawling sensations and foreign body sensation in the right eye, with visual confirmation of a worm.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient reports recent onset of symptoms with no prior ocular complaints. The patient has a history of working in a cocoa farm plantation during childhood and experienced bites from unknown flies. No systemic symptoms or signs were reported besides the ocular complaints. There is no swelling or itching present.

**Ocular Examination:**  
- Visual acuity: 6/6 in both eyes.  
- Significant finding: Presence of a worm in the nasal subconjunctival space of the right eye. The worm migrated away from view during the examination.

**Treatment and Outcome:**  
The patient was taken to the operating room where the worm was successfully extracted using local infiltration of the conjunctiva with lignocaine anesthesia. Histological examination identified the worm as an adult Loa loa worm. The patient reports feeling much better post-extraction.
