**Clinical Note**

**Patient Information:**  
- **Chief Complaint:** Severe pain and swelling in the left hip and upper thigh for the past two days.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient presents with a two-day history of severe, constant, dull aching pain in the left hip and upper thigh, which is exacerbated by movement. The pain persists even at rest. The patient reports swelling in the upper thigh and difficulty walking due to the inability to bear weight on the left lower limb.

**Past Medical History:**  
- Hip hemireplacement operation on the left side 5 years ago.
- No significant past medical history.
- Sedentary worker.
- No smoking or drug use.
- No known allergies or sensitivities to medications.

**Family History:**  
- No family history of tuberculosis or malignancy.

**Physical Examination:**  
- Left lower limb externally rotated.
- Diffuse swelling over the antero-lateral aspect of the upper left thigh.
- Skin overlying the swelling is not warm or inflamed.
- Deep tenderness over the swelling.
- Bony crepitus present.
- Loss of transmitted movements.
- No apparent shortening or true shortening.
- Straight leg raising test negative.
- Distal neurovascular status intact.

**Investigations:**  
- Blood investigations within normal limits.
- Plain radiographs of the left hip and thigh show a Vancouver type B1 periprosthetic fracture with a broken cemented bipolar prosthesis in situ. Sufficient bone stock present, no osteopenia or osteolysis.

**Assessment:**  
The patient has a fracture in the prosthesis.

**Plan:**  
- Surgery planned for internal fixation of the fracture with revision of the prosthesis and reconstruction of the proximal femur with a proximal femoral mega prosthesis.
- The hip will be opened by a posterior approach. The head of the broken prosthesis will be removed easily, but the stem will be well.
- Post-operative rest and physical therapy will be required. Further instructions to be provided post-surgery.
- Surgery scheduled for next week.
