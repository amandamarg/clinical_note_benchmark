**Clinical Note**

**Date:** 10/1/2025  
**Time:** 7:34:17 PM  

**Patient Information:**  
- **Chief Complaint:** Mass in the right breast causing pain and bleeding.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient reports the presence of a mass in the right breast for approximately one year. The mass has been causing pain and bleeding for the past three months. The patient noted that the mass initially decreased in size with homeopathic treatment but subsequently increased in size and is now larger than before.

**Family History:**  
No family history of breast cancer.

**Physical Examination:**  
- A hanging mass in the right breast, ulcerated with an irregular surface and nodular appearance.  
- The mass is attached to underlying structures by a stalk-like process.  
- No nipple discharge or retraction observed.

**Diagnostic Tests:**  
- Blood examination: Slightly elevated ESR, otherwise normal.  
- Ultrasonography: Exophytic growth with heterogeneous echotexture and vascularity.  
- Chest X-rays and abdominal ultrasound: No abnormalities.  
- Fine-needle aspiration cytology: Inconclusive.

**Assessment:**  
The nature and rapid growth of the mass suggest a possible Phyllodes tumor.

**Plan:**  
- Proceed with an excisional biopsy to remove the mass and some surrounding tissue.  
- Follow-up appointment in a few weeks to assess healing and monitor for any changes.

**Patient Instructions:**  
The patient was informed about the procedure and agreed to the plan. Advised to return for follow-up as scheduled.
