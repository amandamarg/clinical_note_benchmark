**Clinical Note:**

**Patient Information:**
- History of lupus
- Previous left knee ACL reconstruction approximately 25 years ago using the Arthrotek bone mulch screw and WasherLoc system
- Tibial WasherLoc removed about 10 years ago

**Procedure:**
- Elective left total knee arthroplasty due to osteoarthritis
- Patient positioned supine
- Standard incision with medial parapatellar arthrotomy performed
- Measured resection technique with intramedullary guide placed in the femur
- Femur cut in 6 degrees of valgus and 3 degrees of external rotation
- Initial placement of size 4 femoral prosthesis noted to overhang medially and laterally on the condyles
- Decision made to downsize femoral component
- 4 in 1 femoral cutting block placed back on femur, in contact with bone mulch ACL screw
- Bone mulch screw located in lateral femoral condyle, cleared with curette, and removed
- Proximal tibia prepared using intramedullary guide with 3 degrees of posterior slope
- Size 3 tibial component and 9 mm poly placed; knee noted to be tight in flexion and extension
- Additional 2 mm resection performed on proximal tibia
- Lateral femoral condyle fracture noted during trialing of prostheses
- Conversion to stemmed femoral component with cruciate stabilizing prosthesis attempted
- Medial femoral condyle fracture noted during trialing
- Intraoperative consultation with adult reconstruction trained orthopaedic surgeon performed
- Immediate surgical correction not possible due to improper implants
- Femoral and tibial canals reamed

**Outcome:**
- Despite best efforts, patient did not survive the surgery

**Family Communication:**
- Family informed of the outcome and expressed gratitude for efforts made
