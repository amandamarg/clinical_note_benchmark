**Clinical Note**

**Patient Information:**
- **Chief Complaint:** Persistent right cheek pain

**Medical History:**
- Allergic rhinitis
- Chronic sinusitis (treated until 3 months ago by an otolaryngologist)

**Family History:**
- Father with severe hearing impairment

**History of Present Illness:**
- Symptoms began 6 weeks ago with high fever, sore throat, and comorbid right cheek pain and mild swelling.
- Patient had a dental checkup the day before the initial visit with no abnormal findings.
- Initially diagnosed with streptococcal infection and suspected facial cellulitis; treatment with amoxicillin improved symptoms.
- Facial pain recurred within 4 weeks of the initial visit.
- Subsequent visit to another department led to suspicion of recurrent cellulitis with sinusitis; cefditoren pivoxil treatment was initiated but pain persisted.

**Diagnosis:**
- Clinically diagnosed with trigeminal neuralgia (TN)

**Plan:**
- Prescribe oral clarithromycin to address sinusitis potentially causing or exacerbating TN.
- Consider other treatment options if clarithromycin is ineffective.

**Follow-Up:**
- Monitor response to clarithromycin and reassess treatment plan if necessary.
