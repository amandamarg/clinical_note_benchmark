**Chief Complaint:**  
Swelling on the left side of the face.

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient presents with a swelling on the left side of the face, which has increased in size over the past month. The swelling is described as uncomfortable and painful. The patient reports a history of tooth extraction 2 months ago due to a badly decayed tooth, which was previously untreated until the pain became unbearable.

**Physical Examination:**  
- Swelling on the left side of the face, extending from the ala region of the nose to the cheek and from the infra-orbital region to the upper part of the mouth, approximately 3 x 2 cm in size.  
- The swelling is firm and slightly painful upon palpation.  
- No palpable lymph nodes.

**Nasal Endoscopic Examination:**  
- Congestion of the left maxillary sinus.  
- Suspected nasal polyp observed.

**Imaging:**  
- Computer tomography reveals abnormal thickening of the left maxillary sinus and a breach in the anterior wall of the maxilla.  
- Presence of a soft tissue mass extending into the facial region.

**Biopsy Results:**  
- Incisional biopsy shows epithelium and connective tissue.  
- Epithelium consists of pseudostratified ciliated columnar cells.  
- Connective tissue contains dense chronic inflammatory cells.

**Assessment:**  
The findings suggest an infection in the maxillary sinus causing the facial swelling.

**Plan:**  
- Initiate antibiotic therapy.  
- Schedule a follow-up appointment in one week to assess the response to treatment.

**Instructions to Patient:**  
The patient was advised to start antibiotics and return for a follow-up in one week. The patient was encouraged to reach out with any questions or concerns.
