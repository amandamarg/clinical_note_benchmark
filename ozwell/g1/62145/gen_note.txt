**Clinical Note**

**Date:** 10/1/2025  
**Time:** 8:15:51 PM  

**Patient Information:**  
- **Chief Complaint:** Pain and swelling in the left temporomandibular joint, fever.  

**History of Present Illness:**  
The patient presented with pain and swelling in the left temporomandibular joint, accompanied by fever. The symptoms began following a tooth extraction performed two weeks prior. The patient reported that the pain was severe and the swelling had been progressively worsening.

**Initial Assessment and Treatment:**  
A CT scan of the neck and facial area was performed, revealing an abscess in the corner of the jawbone. The patient was admitted to the maxillofacial unit, where a surgical drainage of the infected collection was conducted. The treatment included washing of the infected area every 8 hours and intravenous antibiotic therapy. The patient was discharged after 7 days with resolution of symptoms and normalization of analytical parameters.

**Follow-Up Visit:**  
Two weeks post-discharge, the patient returned to the emergency service with recurrent symptoms. The family reported fluctuations in the patient's level of consciousness. Examination revealed swelling of the temporomandibular space, fever, and a fistula with pus output in the preauricular zone. A new CT scan indicated signs of meninges inflammation.

**Consultations and Further Investigations:**  
A consultation with the neurological service was conducted, and a lumbar puncture was performed, confirming bacterial meningitis of dental origin. A brain MRI revealed a temporal subdural empyema.

**Plan:**  
The Neurology and Neurosurgery units have been notified. The patient has been prescribed a triple intravenous antibiotics treatment and surgical washing of the temporomandibular space. The patient has been advised to adhere strictly to the treatment plan and to report any concerns or questions.
